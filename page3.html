Alpha, Beta, and Proxima Centauri

Alpha Centauri (α Centauri, α Cen, or Alpha Cen) is a triple star system in
the southern constellation of Centaurus. It consists of three stars:
Rigil Kentaurus (α Centauri A), Toliman (α Centauri B), and Proxima
Centauri (α Centauri C).[14] Proxima Centauri is the closest star to the
Sun at 4.2465 light-years (1.3020 pc).

Observation

To the naked eye, α Centauri AB appears to be a single star, the brightest
in the southern constellation of Centaurus. Their apparent angular
separation varies over about 80 years between 2 and 22 arcseconds
(the naked eye has a resolution of 60 arcsec), but through much of the orbit,
both are easily resolved in binoculars or small telescopes.
